You need to care about details if you want to do this

It’s easy to take typefaces for granted. It’s no surprise then that we rarely think about what goes into the making of the fonts we use everyday. But maybe we should. I recently designed my first typeface, Bernal Grotesk, and it was a fun challange but harder than I expected.

01 Process

Sketching letters

I started sketching by hand, and early on I sketched letters of different neighborhoods in San Francisco. Bernal Heights is one of my favorite neighborhoods, which became my inspiration for naming this typeface. I did most of the digital design in Illustrator and then I continued in Glyphs to work on details and metrics.
